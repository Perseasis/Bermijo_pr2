Paula Scher is a renowned American graphic designer, painter, and educator, widely recognized for her innovative use of typography and her impactful contributions to visual culture. She earned her BFA from the Tyler School of Art at Temple University in 1970.
Scher began her career at CBS Records, where she created album covers for artists such as Bob Dylan and Bruce Springsteen. In the 1980s, she joined the design firm Pentagram, becoming the first female partner in its New York office. Her work there has included branding projects for major institutions like The Public Theater and the Citibank logo.

A pioneer in the field of graphic design, Scher is celebrated for her bold use of type and color, often employing a unique blend of traditional and contemporary styles. Her influential posters for the Public Theater helped redefine the visual identity of theater marketing.

In addition to her design work, Scher is a passionate educator, having taught at institutions such as the School of Visual Arts and the Royal College of Art in London. She has received numerous awards, including the AIGA Gold Medal and the National Design Award from the Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum.
